If you look at a satellite map of Santa Clara, you see a sea of asphalt surrounding Levi's Stadium. These are the corporate campuses of the Silicon Valley giants—Cisco, Intel, and the various tech parks along Tasman Drive.

To the uninitiated fan, this looks like overflow parking heaven. You might think, "It's Sunday, the offices are closed, I'll just slide into that massive empty lot on Champion Court."

Do not do this. You will be towed before you even reach the stadium gates.

## The Liability Lockdown
Here is the reality of the 95054 commercial zone during Super Bowl week. These tech companies do not see fans as revenue opportunities; they see them as liability risks.

I spoke with a security supervisor for one of the major tech firms on Tasman. Their orders for Sunday are simple: **Zero Tolerance.**

They aren't just locking the gates. They have hired third-party security contractors (mostly Allied Universal and Securitas) to sit in patrol cars at the entrances of *empty* lots. Their sole job is to wave you off. If you manage to sneak in through a back entrance or a broken gate, the roving tow trucks—which are staged nearby specifically for this purpose—will have your car in the impound lot within 15 minutes.

## The "Leased" Trap
Some of these lots *will* have cars in them, which makes it even more confusing. The NFL has quietly leased specific corporate lots for:
*   Media overflow
*   Stadium staff parking
*   Halftime show production crews

These lots require specific color-coded dashboard placards. If you see a lot with cars and an open gate, it is not a free-for-all. It is a credentialed zone. If you park there without the placard, you aren't just getting a ticket; you are getting towed to a lot in San Jose that closes at 5:00 PM.

## The Employee Badge Myth
I've heard locals say, "My cousin works at Cisco, I'll just use his badge."

Not this week. The campuses inside the "Traffic Management Perimeter" are on lockdown even for employees. Unless that employee is critical infrastructure (server maintenance, security), their badge will not open the gate. The readers are often disabled or overridden by physical security guards who are checking a specific "Essential Access" list.

## The Safe Play
Stop looking for a loophole in the office parks. The days of parking at a random office building on Great America Parkway are gone.

If you don't have a prepaid stadium pass, your only safe "guerrilla" parking options are:
1.  **The Amtrak/ACE Lot:** It's public, but it fills up by 9:00 AM.
2.  **Mission College:** It's a hike, but it's legitimate paid parking.
3.  **Private Driveways:** Look for residents holding cardboard signs on the south side of Agnew. Cash only, usually $50-$100. It feels sketchy, but it's safer than a corporate lot because the homeowner actually wants you there.
