If you see a missed field goal on Sunday that looks like it was kicked with a brick, don't blame the wind. Blame the box.

## The Sealed Shipment
While the quarterbacks have spent the last week massaging, brushing, and breaking in the 12 primary game balls to their exact tactile preference, the kicking balls (K-Balls) are currently sitting in a sealed box in the referee's locker room at Levi's Stadium.

These balls were shipped directly from the Wilson factory in Ada, Ohio. They have not been touched by a human hand since they were boxed.

## The "Rock" Factor
Here is the specific protocol that drives kickers crazy:
1.  **The Unboxing:** The Referee crew opens the box 2 hours and 15 minutes before kickoff.
2.  **The Marking:** They mark them with a "K" to ensure they don't get mixed into the quarterback rotation.
3.  **The Hardness:** Because they haven't been "worked" (rubbed with mud, twisted, or battered), the leather is tight and waxy. The seams are higher. The ball compresses less on impact.

## The Warmup Window
Kickers and punters are given access to the K-Balls roughly 45 minutes before kickoff. They are allowed to kick them. They are **not** allowed to modify them.

You will see equipment managers frantically rubbing the balls with towels, but they are prohibited from using water, brushes, or softening agents. If an official catches a ball boy trying to break the spine of a K-Ball, that ball is removed from play.

## The Impact on SB60
Levi's Stadium is already a heavy air environment at sea level. Combine the dense air with a waxy, hard K-Ball, and you lose about 3-5 yards of effective range.

Watch the trajectory on 50+ yard attempts. If the ball knuckles or dies abruptly at the crossbar, it's the K-Ball effect. The margin for error on the sweet spot shrinks from a quarter-sized area to a dime.
