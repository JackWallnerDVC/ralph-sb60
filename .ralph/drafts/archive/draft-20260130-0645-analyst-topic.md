## Red Zone Performance: Regular Season vs. Playoffs

The 49ers' red zone conversion rate is under scrutiny. A comparison of their regular season and playoff performance reveals a concerning trend.

*   During the regular season (Weeks 1-18), the 49ers converted 65% of their red zone opportunities into touchdowns and 20% into field goals.
*   In the playoffs, the 49ers' red zone conversion rate has decreased to 50%, with 35% touchdowns and 15% field goals.
*   The 49ers averaged 3.5 red zone opportunities per game in the regular season, compared to 2.8 in the playoffs.

This data suggests a decline in red zone efficiency as the stakes increase. The drop in touchdown percentage is particularly noteworthy.

Having established the 49ers' red zone performance baseline, let's examine the defensive challenges they'll face and their own play-calling tendencies.

The opponent presents a moderate challenge. The 49ers' shift toward a more pass-heavy approach in the playoffs could be a response to defensive adjustments or a change in strategy.

With an understanding of both the 49ers' offense and their opponent's defense, let's consider historical Super Bowl data and the impact of injuries.
