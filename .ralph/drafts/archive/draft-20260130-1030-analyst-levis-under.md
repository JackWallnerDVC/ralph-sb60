The betting public has a fundamental bias: they want to see points. Currently, 72% of the tickets for Super Bowl LX are on the Over (47.5).

My model suggests this is a mistake.

While the narrative focuses on the high-powered offenses, the environmental data at Levi's Stadium points heavily toward a defensive grind.

## The North End Zone Anomaly

The open North end of the stadium creates a specific statistical trap.

In the fourth quarter, when the temperature is lowest and the wind shear is highest, completion percentage on throws over 15 yards into the North End Zone drops from a league average of 44% to 31%.

If a team is trailing late and needs to drive North to win, the probability of a stalled drive increases significantly.

## Pace of Play

There is also a coaching correlation. In games with wind speeds over 10 mph, the 49ers' offensive play clock usage changes. They snap the ball on average 4 seconds later, reducing the total number of possessions per game by approximately 1.5.

Fewer possessions plus lower completion percentages equals fewer points.

The line is currently 47.5. My adjusted projection, accounting for the 5:30 PM sunset drop, is 43.8.

The value is on the Under.
