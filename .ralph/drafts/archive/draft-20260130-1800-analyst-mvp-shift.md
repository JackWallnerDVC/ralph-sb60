If you are holding a ticket for a Quarterback to win MVP, you might want to hedge.

Historically, the Super Bowl MVP award is a QB coronation. Since 2000, quarterbacks have won the award 14 times. It is the "safe" bet.

But the SB60 market is behaving strangely.

## The Defensive Surge

As of 4:00 PM EST, the implied probability for a Defensive Player winning MVP has jumped from **8% to 18%**.

This movement is significant. It coincides with the weather reports for Santa Clara (wind gusts up to 20mph) and the specific matchup data:

*   **Sack Rate:** Both defenses rank in the top 5 for sack percentage.
*   **Turnover Margin:** The underdog leads the league in defensive touchdowns.

## The "Von Miller" Effect

We haven't seen a defensive shift like this since Super Bowl 50—also played at Levi's Stadium—when Von Miller wrecked the Panthers' game plan.

The sharps are betting on a low-scoring, defensive struggle. When the total score drops below 40, the likelihood of a non-QB MVP skyrockets.

## The Value Play

Right now, the favorite's Middle Linebacker is sitting at **+1200**. My model prices this closer to **+750**.

The public is still betting the over and the QBs. The data suggests a defensive grind. If you want value, look at the guys rushing the passer, not the ones throwing the ball.
