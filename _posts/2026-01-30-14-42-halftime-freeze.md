If you are live-betting the Super Bowl, beware the "Over" in the third quarter.

In the regular season, halftime lasts 12 minutes. It is just enough time for players to use the restroom, make a quick adjustment, and get back on the field. Their heart rates stay elevated. Their muscles stay loose.

In the Super Bowl, halftime is a 29-minute theatrical production. And the data suggests this extended break wreaks havoc on offensive rhythm.

## The Cooling Effect
Physiologically, a 30-minute break is a disaster for an athlete in peak exertion. Lactic acid settles. Adrenaline spikes crash.

Looking at the last 10 Super Bowls, the opening drive of the second half results in a punt or turnover 18% more often than the league average for regular season games. The offenses come out "cold."

## The Script Reset
Offensive coordinators typically script their first 15 plays of the game. By the third quarter, they are relying on adjustments.

However, the extended halftime allows Defensive Coordinators significantly more time to diagnose the first half. In a standard game, a DC has about 6 minutes of actual whiteboard time with the defense. In the Super Bowl, they have nearly 20 minutes.

This asymmetry favors the defense. It is easier to destroy a game plan than to create a new one on the fly.

## The Betting Angle
The "Third Quarter Under" has hit in 7 of the last 10 Super Bowls. The market often prices the total evenly across quarters, failing to account for the "restart tax" that teams pay coming out of the tunnel.

Expect a sluggish start to the second half. The fireworks usually wait until the fourth quarter, when urgency overrides the stiffness.
