I see it every time the 49ers play a primetime game. Fans from out of town show up in t-shirts and shorts because the weather app says "68°F and Sunny" at kickoff.

By the third quarter, they are shivering uncontrollably and buying $90 hoodies from the team store just to survive.

## The "Alviso" Effect

Levi's Stadium isn't in San Francisco. It's in Santa Clara, specifically on the edge of the San Francisco Bay wetlands (Alviso).

We have a very specific microclimate here.
1.  **Kickoff (3:30 PM):** The sun is blazing. If you are on the East side (Sections 100-200), you are baking.
2.  **Sunset (5:30 PM):** The sun dips behind the massive luxury suite tower on the West side. The stadium falls into shadow.
3.  **The Drop:** The temperature doesn't just slide; it crashes. We often see a 15-20 degree drop in 90 minutes.

## The Wind Tunnel

As the land cools, the breeze from the Bay picks up. Because the North end of the stadium is open, that cold, damp air funnels right through the bowl.

It’s not a dry cold. It’s a damp, bone-chilling marine layer cold. 50°F in Santa Clara feels colder than 40°F in New York because of the humidity.

## The Local's Strategy

**Do not wear just a jersey.**

You need the "Bay Area Tuxedo":
*   **Base Layer:** T-shirt.
*   **Mid Layer:** Flannel or hoodie.
*   **Outer Layer:** A windbreaker or shell. This is crucial. The wind is the enemy.

If you are sitting in the **400 level**, bring a beanie. I'm serious. The wind shear up there is no joke.

Don't let the palm trees fool you. When the sun goes down, the Bay bites back.
